Your payment in this task consists of two parts:

  1. One part of your payment will be determined by the quality of your prediction of B's willingness to pay. The closer your prediction is to B's actually reported willingness to pay, the higher your payment. (Prediction score)
  2. Another part of your payment will be determined by the quality of B's prediction of C's willingness to pay. The closer B's prediction is to C's actually reported willingness to pay, the higher your payment. (Information score)

So, you increase your payment not only by making a good prediction about B, but also by providing B with your true willingness to pay. In fact, with this method your expected payment will be highest if you report your willingness to pay truthfully.

For each part the minimum payment is {{min_pay}}. This minimum occurs if the actual willingness to pay was predicted to be "Not at all" likely. The maximum payment per part is {{max_pay}}. This will occur if the actual willingness to pay was correctly predicted (i.e. to be "very likely" and all other possible answers were predicted to be "Not at all" likely). For predictions between these two extremes each score takes a value between {{min_pay}} and {{max_pay}}.
